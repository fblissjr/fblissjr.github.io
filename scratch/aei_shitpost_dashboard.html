The Automation Ladder

Automation vs. augmentation by GDP quartile. Poor countries: "just do it." Rich countries: "let's discuss."
The automation-to-augmentation gradient is one of the most striking findings. Countries with avg GDP under $3K use Claude 70% for pure automation—directive, fire-and-forget tasks. Countries above $85K average are essentially 50/50, treating Claude as a collaborative partner. This isn't just a feature preference; it may reflect fundamentally different labor economics. When labor is cheap, you automate the expensive stuff. When labor is expensive, you augment the humans you've already got.
